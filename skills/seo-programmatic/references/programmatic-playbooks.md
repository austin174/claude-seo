# Programmatic SEO Playbooks

12 proven playbook types for building pages at scale. Use this to select the right approach based on the client's data assets and business model.

## The 12 Playbook Types

### 1. Templates
**Pattern:** "[Type] template" or "free [type] template"
**Examples:** resume template, invoice template, pitch deck template
**Why it works:** High intent, shareable assets, natural for product-led companies
**URL:** `/templates/[type]/` or `/templates/[category]/[type]/`
**Value gate:** Actually usable templates, multiple variations per type, quality comparable to paid options

### 2. Curation
**Pattern:** "best [category]" or "top [number] [things]"
**Examples:** best website builders, top 10 CRM software
**Why it works:** Comparison shoppers, high commercial intent, evergreen with updates
**URL:** `/best/[category]/` or `/[category]/best/`
**Value gate:** Genuine evaluation criteria, real testing, regular updates (visible date)

### 3. Conversions
**Pattern:** "[X] to [Y]" or "[amount] [unit] in [unit]"
**Examples:** $10 USD to GBP, 100 kg to lbs, PDF to Word
**Why it works:** Instant utility, high search volume, repeat usage
**URL:** `/convert/[from]-to-[to]/` or `/[from]-to-[to]-converter/`
**Value gate:** Accurate real-time data, fast functional tool, mobile-friendly

### 4. Comparisons
**Pattern:** "[X] vs [Y]" or "[X] alternative"
**Examples:** Webflow vs WordPress, Notion vs Coda, Figma alternatives
**Why it works:** High purchase intent, clear pattern, scales with competitors
**URL:** `/compare/[x]-vs-[y]/` or `/[x]-vs-[y]/`
**Value gate:** Honest balanced analysis, actual feature data, clear recommendation by use case

### 5. Examples
**Pattern:** "[type] examples" or "[category] inspiration"
**Examples:** SaaS landing page examples, email subject line examples
**Why it works:** Research phase traffic, highly shareable, natural for creative tools
**URL:** `/examples/[type]/` or `/[type]-examples/`
**Value gate:** Real high-quality examples, screenshots/embeds, analysis of why they work

### 6. Locations
**Pattern:** "[service/thing] in [location]"
**Examples:** coworking spaces in San Diego, dentists in Austin
**Why it works:** Local intent is massive, scales with geography
**URL:** `/[service]/[city]/` or `/locations/[city]/[service]/`
**Value gate:** Actual local data (not city-name swaps), local providers listed, location-specific insights

### 7. Personas
**Pattern:** "[product] for [audience]" or "[solution] for [role/industry]"
**Examples:** payroll software for agencies, CRM for real estate
**Why it works:** Speaks directly to context, higher conversion than generic pages
**URL:** `/for/[persona]/` or `/solutions/[industry]/`
**Value gate:** Genuine persona-specific content, relevant features, testimonials from that segment

### 8. Integrations
**Pattern:** "[your product] [other product] integration"
**Examples:** Slack Asana integration, Zapier Airtable
**Why it works:** Captures users of other products, high intent, scales with ecosystem
**URL:** `/integrations/[product]/` or `/connect/[product]/`
**Value gate:** Real integration details, setup instructions, working integration (not vaporware)

### 9. Glossary
**Pattern:** "what is [term]" or "[term] definition"
**Examples:** what is pSEO, API definition
**Why it works:** Top-of-funnel awareness, establishes expertise, internal linking opportunities
**URL:** `/glossary/[term]/` or `/learn/[term]/`
**Value gate:** Clear accurate definitions, examples, related terms linked, more depth than a dictionary

### 10. Translations
**Pattern:** Same content in multiple languages
**Why it works:** Opens new markets, lower competition, multiplies reach
**URL:** `/[lang]/[page]/` or `yoursite.com/es/`, `/de/`
**Value gate:** Quality translation (not machine-only), cultural localization, hreflang implemented, native speaker review

### 11. Directory
**Pattern:** "[category] tools" or "[type] software"
**Examples:** AI copywriting tools, email marketing software
**Why it works:** Research phase capture, link building magnet
**URL:** `/directory/[category]/` or `/[category]-directory/`
**Value gate:** Comprehensive coverage, useful filtering, details per listing, regular updates

### 12. Profiles
**Pattern:** "[person/company name]" or "[entity] + [attribute]"
**Examples:** Stripe CEO, Airbnb founding story
**Why it works:** Informational intent, builds topical authority
**URL:** `/people/[name]/` or `/companies/[name]/`
**Value gate:** Accurate sourced information, regularly updated, unique insights

## Data Defensibility Hierarchy

When evaluating a programmatic SEO strategy, the data source determines long-term defensibility:

| Tier | Type | Defensibility | Example |
|------|------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | **Proprietary** | Highest — you created it | Internal research, proprietary algorithms |
| 2 | **Product-derived** | High — from your users | Usage analytics, aggregate benchmarks |
| 3 | **User-generated** | Medium-high — community creates | Reviews, forum posts, submitted listings |
| 4 | **Licensed/Enriched** | Medium — exclusive access | API data with analysis layer |
| 5 | **Curated** | Medium-low — editorial layer on public data | Handpicked recommendations with expert commentary |
| 6 | **Aggregated** | Low — anyone can replicate | Scraped public data, compiled statistics |

**Rule:** If your data source is Tier 5-6, you need significantly more unique editorial value per page to avoid thin content classification. Tier 1-3 data inherently creates unique pages.

## Choosing Your Playbook

| If you have... | Consider... |
|----------------|-------------|
| Proprietary data | Stats, Directories, Profiles |
| Product with integrations | Integrations |
| Design/creative product | Templates, Examples |
| Multi-segment audience | Personas |
| Local presence | Locations |
| Tool or utility product | Conversions |
| Content/expertise | Glossary, Curation |
| International potential | Translations |
| Competitor landscape | Comparisons |

### Combining Playbooks

Layer multiple approaches for compound value:
- **Locations + Personas:** "Marketing agencies for startups in Austin"
- **Curation + Locations:** "Best coworking spaces in San Diego"
- **Integrations + Personas:** "Slack for sales teams"
- **Glossary + Translations:** Multi-language educational content
- **Directory + Locations:** "[Category] companies in [city]"
